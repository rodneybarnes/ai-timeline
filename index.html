Timeline of AI in Montreal

2 May, 1670

King Charles II grants the Hudson’s Bay Company with a Royal Charter

The Royal Charter gives the company a monopoly and control over lands whose rivers and streams drain into Hudson Bay. This land, which was, and still is, unceded Indigenous territory, stretched from Labrador to the Rocky Mountains, and south to the American border.

1763

The British sets up a military government in Quebec

Following the Seven Years’ War, the British set up a military government in Quebec, and sign the Royal Proclamation, which gives the British monarch the power to purchase and sell Indigenous land

1868

Britain "purchases" Rupert's Land

In 1868, Britain buys Rupert’s land (which was “owned” by the Hudson's Bay Company) for 300,000 pounds. The HBC is given control over 20% of the fertile land. Indigenous groups, including the Inuit, Métis and First Nations groups in the area are not consulted on the purchase

23 June, 1870

The Rupert's Land Order is Signed

As a result of the signing of the Order, Rupert’s Land is incorporated into the Dominion of Canada. Term 8, established in the negotiations of 1868-69 states: “It is understood that any claims of Indians to compensation for lands required for purposes of settlement shall be disposed of by the Canadian Government, in communication with the Imperial Government — and that the Company shall be relieved of all responsibility in respect of them.” Term 14 of the Deed of Surrender states: “​​Any claims of Indians to compensation for lands required for purposes of settlement shall be disposed of by the Canadian Government in communication with the Imperial Government; and the Company shall be relieved of all responsibility in respect of them.” While these terms recognized Indigenous title, it is misleading — no Indigneous groups took part in these negotiations.

1 April, 1912

The Boundary Extension Act is Created

On April 1 1912, the Quebec Boundaries are extended to include James Bay, with the condition that prior to any development of the area, the province of Quebec must “recognize the rights of the Indian inhabitants in the territory above described to the same extent, and will obtain surrenders of such rights in the same manner, as the Government of Canada has heretofore recognized such rights and has obtained surrender thereof, and the said province shall bear and satisfy all charges and expenditures in connection with or arising out of such surrenders.”

12 June, 1962

The Société Générale de Financement is Created in Quebec

The Quebec government enacts a law that founds a corporation that exists to promote economic development in the province, especially in the industrial sector.

8 April, 1971

The Société de Développement Industriel du Québec is Founded

The Quebec government creates an economic development company, that aims to "develop profitable economic activities which generate significant economic benefits in Quebec, particularly as regards job creation."

30 April, 1971

The James Bay Hydroelectric Dam Project is Announced

Quebec Premier Robert Bourassa announces the James Bay Project — a plan to construct a multi-million dollar dam in James Bay. The plan ignored how construction of the dam would impact the 5,000-6,000 Cree and 3,500 Inuit people in the area.

15 November, 1973

Cree and Inuit Communities are Granted an Injunction to Stop the Construction of the James Bay Project

Justice Albert Malouf of the Quebec Superior Court grants an injunction to stop the construction of the James Bay Project. This injunction is turned over a week later, but this decision represents a victory for the Cree and Inuit communities.

11 November, 1975

The James Bay and Northern Quebec Agreement is Signed

The James Bay and Northern Quebec Agreement (JBNQA) is signed. It was the first major comprehensive land claims agreement in Canada. The agreement is signed by the Government of Quebec, the James Bay Energy Corporation, the James Bay Development Corporation, Hydro-Québec, the Grand Council of the Crees, the Northern Quebec Inuit Association, and the Government of Canada. The James Bay Cree and Inuit of Northern Quebec are provided with $225 million in compensation through the JBQNA, paid by the federal and provincial government.

1980

Quebec Starts to Offer Tax Breaks to Technology Companies

Quebec begins instituting refundable tax credits in the 1980s for scientific research and experimental development (R&D) oriented towards small and medium-sized businesses, which significantly offsets the cost of salaries of people working at technology companies.

1982

University of Toronto Professor founds the Canadian Institute for Advanced Research (CIFAR)

CIFAR, an organization that aims to bring together “the world's best scholars and scientists to address high-risk, important, and complex questions”, hires University of Toronto professor Geoffrey Hinton in 1987 after launching its Robotics and Society program in 1983.

1986

Computer Graphics and Animation Firm Softimage is founded in Montreal

The graphics and animation firm becomes an incubator for other computer graphics development companies, like Discreet and Kaydara in the early 1990s. Softimage and other computer graphics firms play an essential role in building a digital animation culture and providing the foundation for the creation of an economic cluster of video game work in Montreal.

November, 1989

Le Comité D’action de Parc-Extension (CAPE) was Formed

CAPE was formed with the goals of addressing public health and neighbourhood development. Projects taken on by the CAPE included housing, street cleaning, park improvement, community development, and support for immigrants in Parc Ex. In 1991, CAPE narrowed its mandate to focus specifically on tenant rights, and accessibility to housing. Currently they focus on supporting the residents of Parc Ex’s resistances to incoming gentrification due to mass influx of tech companies in the neighbourhood.

1996

Quebec Provides More Refundable Tax Credits for Technology Companies in Montreal

From 1996 to 2001, the provincial government continues to provide refundable tax credits for salaries and human resources concerns for the production of multimedia titles (1996), for tangible goods acquired as a result of technology transfer (patents, licenses, permits, knowhow, trade secrets, 1998), and for the establishment of corporations within certain geographic parts of Montreal, among others. Many of these tax credits are governed/regulated/vetted by the province’s investment arm Investissement Québec.

1993

Yoshua Bengio Begins teaching at Université de Montréal and Founds LISA labs

In 1993, Université de Montréal hires Yoshua Bengio as a computer science professor. Bengio also founds a research lab in the computer science department called LISA (Laboratoire d'Informatique des Systèmes d’Apprentissage) that same year.

June, 1994

Lou Montulli Invents the HTTP Cookie

The invention of the HTTP cookie alters the way privacy on the internet functions, with its use becoming a new way to store internet activity. With the implementation of web cookies, data from interactions within the internet sphere are able to be stored, mined and even sold.

1996

Montreal International is founded

Montreal International is founded. It is a private-public partnership that has the mission to “attract foreign investment, international organizations and strategic talent to the metropolitan region, as well as to promote the competitive environment of Greater Montréal.”

February, 1996

The Financial Times publishes "The bug in your PC is a smart cookie"

This article, first published in February 1996, represents one of the first articles about the dangers of web cookies as a threat to privacy on the internet. It notes how the blackboxing of the HTTP cookie makes it so that people are not only unaware that they exist, but also that internet users don't have control over how their data is used.

25 April, 1997

Ubisoft arrives in Montreal

Ubisoft, a French game development company, moves to Montreal to benefit from the video game economic cluster and to draw on the pool of creative talent in the city.

1997

The Quebec government introduces the Centres de développement des technologies de l'information (CDTI) program

The program, run with the support of the provincial economic development and funding body, aims to "promote the creation of technological clusters to maximize synergies and collaboration" and "ensure that companies settle in a building adapted to their needs."

1998

The Société de développement industriel du Québec becomes Investissement Québec

The Société de développement industriel du Québec founded in 1971 is transformed and renamed as Investissement Québec. The goals of the corporation are to stimulate innovation in enterprises, entrepreneurship, and acquisition; to stimulate the growth of investments and exports; and to promote high-value-added jobs across the province.

15 June, 1998

The Quebec government creates the Cité du Multimédia and offers wage subsidies

The Quebec government provides $360 million in funding to create a multimedia tech hub in a former industrial part of Montreal. The government also introduces a 25% wage subsidy and other incentives for jobs created in the “Multimedia City” — with a goal to have 10,000 jobs in the city by 2008.

2000

Developments in Graphics Processing Units Influence Artificial Intelligence Development

CAN WE MAKE THIS MORE SPECIFIC???Developments in graphics processing units or GPUs allow artificial intelligence and big data processing techniques to be implemented at significantly increased speeds, broadly corresponding to the creation of enormous datasets and benchmark challenges particularly for those related to computer vision such as ImageNet

12 June, 2000

Gameloft opens their Montreal Office

Paris-based videogame development company Gameloft opens an office in Montreal. The company aims to create 400 jobs in Montreal, adding to “the city's booming software business scene”

23 October, 2000

Google Launches AdWords program

The program is designed to enabled advertisers to purchase keyword advertising on the Google search page. This represents a shift towards an overall business model based on advertising, enabling clients to model the preformance of their ads directly through the Google website. With the program, Google offers specific afforances, such as instant appearance on the site, preview tools, unlimited ad creation, advanced keyword targeting options, web-based reporting tools and performance feedback of ads.

2004

The Neural Computation and Adaptive Perception Group is launched

In 2004, CIFAR launches a research group focused on reviving interest in neural networks, called the Neural Computation and Adaptive Perception (now called Learning in Machines and Brains), led by Geoffrey Hinton, including members Yoshua Bengio and Yann LeCun

2006

Yann Lecun and others attempts to rekindle interest in neural networks

In 2006, Yann Lecun, Facebook’s head of AI, refers to efforts by himself, Geoffrey Hinton, and Yoshua Bengio to rekindle interest in neural networks (a.k.a. deep learning), which sum dub “the deep learning conspiracy.”

22 February, 2006

iWeb announces the creation of a Data Centre on the Island of Montreal

The data center was developed in Saint-Leonard's industrial park and established following an 88% growth in 2005. The project was estimated to cost approximately $15 million, with the plan to combine revenue from iWeb's other data centre for an estimated annual revenue of $42 million. The centre will host almost 20,000 servers.

2 October, 2007

iWeb announces the development of a third data centre

The facility, which was previously a data centre itself, already has the basic infrastructure necessary, and is planned to act as a hosting colocation data center, which will "enable us to diversitfy our revenue base, extend our footprint in the Montreal marketplace and target a broader customer base"

2008

Video game boom creates fear of gentrification among Mile End artists

Throughout 2008, the booming tech industry in Mile End instilled a fear for artists who had lived in the area. Allied Properties Real Estate Investment Trust purchased the Peck Building alongside two others for $30 million more than they had cost four years earlier. Following this purchase, a strategic alliance between residents, businesses and local politicians advocated for a protection of rent. In 2012, the borough passed a zoning law to restrict the size of commercial tenants in megastructures. However, Allied didn't use this as an opportunity for reviewing tenants leases, rather they pushed them out to make way for smaller commercial businesses.

9 March, 2009

Canada Media Fund is Announced

On March 9 2009, Minister of Heritage James Moore announces the Canada Media Fund (CMF) is announced to “help Canadian content creators get the support needed to bring their ideas to life.” The Canada Media Fund was created as a result of a merger between the Canada Television Fund and the Canadian New Media Fund, taking investments from the government of Canada and cable, satellite, and IPTV distributors and investing in Canadian audiovisual creation including TV shows, video games, web series, virtual reality, interactive digital media and software applications.

2011

Notman House launches as startup hub

A heritage building in Montréal, the Notman House, is restored and turned into a startup hub in the city

28 May, 2012

The OVH data centre opens in Quebec

This centre is the first OVH data centre on the American continent, and will be powered by hydroelectricity from a dam 300 metres away from the building.

2013

Geoffrey Hinton, Yann Lecun and Yoshua Bengio work on new projects

Geoffrey Hinton is hired as a distinguished researcher at Google, Yann Lecun is hired to work on AI at Facebook, and IBM announces it’s working with Yoshua Bengio focused on incorporating deep learning into Watson

3 June, 2013

Ericsson announces $1.2 billion information technology and data centre

On June 2 2013, Swedish-based communications technology company Ericsson announces a plan to spend $1.2-billion to build a new information technology and data centre in Quebec.The company planned to open a 40,000 square-metre (the size of eight football fields) centre in Vaudreuil-Dorion. The provincial government agreed to provide up to $30 million in financial support, as well as “preferential electricity rates”. Premier Pauline Marois also confirmed that Ericsson could be eligible for a 10-year income-tax holiday for companies that make an investment of at least $300 million into the province.

30 October, 2013

Internap acquires iWeb for $145 million

Internap Network Services Corporation acquired the cloud provider alongside its data centres. In response to the acquisition, Internap assured that "the combined customer base will benefit from one of the most compelling bare-metal, cloud and hybrid hosting portfolios in the industry"

14 January, 2014

COLOGIX opens their seventh data centre in Montreal

The data centre is located in Montreal at 1155 Rue University. The opening of this data centre is a part of a broader project for COLOGIX to expand its data centre space across Canada, with other centers opening in Vancouver.

11 March, 2014

ROOT announces plans to build a data centre in Quebec

The data centre will offer the world's cheapest colocation at a price 30-70% less than its local and international peers. "We are building a world class facility without the world class price tag."

10 June, 2014

Yoshua Bengio and Ian Goodfellow, among many others, release a paper on generative adversarial networks

2014

NCAP is rebranded as Leaning in Machines and Brains

CIFAR’s Neural Computation & Adaptive Perception (NCAP) is renamed Learning in Machines & Brains with Yoshua Bengio and Yann LeCun appointed as co-directors.

13 February, 2015

Cogeco announces plan to open massive data centre

The telecommunications company states that the data centre will be 100,000 square feet.

9 March, 2015

IBM opens a new data centre in Quebec

IBM states that creating this new centre "will allow Québec-based organizations to achieve a new level of application performance and responsiveness by providing customers with a local facility for low-latency connections as well as the ability for in-country data redundancy in both Montréal and Toronto"

15 March, 2015

Videotron acquires 4Degrees Colocation and its Quebec city data centre for $31 million

“The company's Quebec colocation data center is a large, modern facility. Built just last year in the Quebec Metro High Tech Park, it is Uptime Institute Tier III-certified with room for 2,000 cabinets or 85,000 servers.”

June, 2015

Yoshua Bengio's LISA Lab appears to cease existing; replaced with MILA, a new Université de Montréal lab

Yoshua Bengio’s LISA Lab appears to cease existing and a new Université de Montréal lab, Montréal Institute for Learning Algorithms (MILA or Mila) begins to be routinely mentioned. For example, Ian Goodfellow publicly referred to LISA in March 2015, but people begin using MILA as their affiliation for various public outputs as early as June 2015.

18 January, 2016

Amazon Web Services announces plan for a new Canadian data centrre

On January 18 2016, Amazon Web Services announces the opening of its first Canadian data centre, with Montreal being its first Canadian region for its data centres.

8 June, 2016

Hydro-Quebec announces a move to attract more data centres to Quebec.

Specifically, Hydro-Quebec offers: 1) Competitive rates, including the Economic Development Rate (EDR), 2) A portfolio of appropriate sites, representing over 25 million sf and located in strategic areas, 3) Support to get projects up and running quickly and 4) Energy efficient expertise to help reduce energy consumption

June, 2016

Yoshua Bengio becomes scientific director of IVADO

Bengio becomes scientific director of IVADO. The Institut de Valorisation des Données (IVADO) incorporated as a non-profit in 2017 and in existence since at least July 2015, is led by HEC Montréal, Polytechnique Montréal, and Université de Montréal. It seeks to advance economic and technological development on AI in Montréal by bridging the private and public tech spheres. IVADO brings together "six world-class research centres (GERAD, CIRRELT, Mila, CRM, the Canada Excellence Research Chair in Data Science for Real-Time Decision Making and Tech3lab). The institute is funded by Canada First Research Excellence Fund, the Ministère de l’Économie et de l’Innovation (MEI), the Fonds de recherche du Québec (FRQ) and “several industrial members”

July, 2016

Ivado recieves over $110 million in funding

As of July 2016, IVADO has thus far received more than $110 million CAD from “Google, Facebook, General Electric, Hydro-Québec, the Société nationale des chemin de fer français, Air Canada, Gaz Métro, CAE, National Bank, Microsoft, Merck, IBM, Caisse de depot et placement as well as Power Corporation, owner of La Presse.

6 September, 2016

Canada’s federal Innovation, Science and Economic Development department grants $900 million CAD to 13 post-secondary institutions in Canada as part of its Canada First Research Excellence Fund

Within this grant, Montreal universities land a historic $213M CAD investment for computer and brain research. Université de Montréal, McGill University, Polytechnique Montréal and HEC Montréal announced $213 million from the Canada First Research Excellence Fund for three innovative and far-reaching projects. Specifically, $93.5 million is allocated to create IVADO. Yoshua Bengio, when asked about the investment, states: “With the money, U de M, Polytechnique and HEC plan to create IVADO, an institute to advance artificial intelligence (AI) and big data with a goal of creating computers with quasi-human-level performance. It will focus on the science of optimization”. He also emphasizes that the investment would help create “a mini Silicon Valley" in Montreal.

13 September, 2016

Videotron opens its 4Degrees data centre in Montreal

This datacenter, which was acquired by Videotron in 2015, cost $40 million to construct, and was redesigned specifically for data hosting. This center is eventually acquired by Vantage in 2019 following the purchase of 4Degress from Videotron.

25 October, 2016

Yoshua Bengio launches Element AI

On October 25 2016, Wired announces that Yoshua Bengio is launching Element AI, “a Silicon Valley-style startup incubator.” Element AI was founded by Anne Martel, Gabriel Duford, Jean-Francois Gagne, Jean-Sebastian Cournoyer, Nicolas Chapados, Philippe Beaudoin, and Yoshua Bengio. Newswire states that Element AI, founded by Bengio along with serial entrepreneurs Jean-François Gagné and Nicolas Chapados, is “an artificial intelligence startup factory.” News of the company’s launch is also announced at the Notman House in Montréal.

21 November, 2016

Google invests $4.5 million into MILA

Google states it will invest $4.5 million “with an academic fund covering three years that will help pay for seven faculty members across various Montréal academic institutions.” The new AI team “will be a remote arm of its Google Brain team based in Mountain View, and will be led locally by Hugo Larochelle, a deep learning expert who’s returning home to Montreal from a role with Twitter in Boston specifically for the new position.”

21 November, 2016

Google announces opening of the Google Brain AI lab in Montreal

The new AI team “will be a remote arm of its Google Brain team based in Mountain View, and will be led locally by Hugo Larochelle, a deep learning expert who’s returning home to Montreal from a role with Twitter in Boston specifically for the new position.”

10 December, 2016

Ericsson’s data centre opens

The centre is half of its projected size, at 20,000 square metres. The facility was created to serve as “a full-scale emulation facility for prospective, future telecommunications networks.”

12 December, 2016

Microsoft creates a venture capital fund dedicated to AI research

The first investment they make is in Element AI (amount undisclosed). This follows Google $4.5 million investment, and announcement of an AI lab in Montreal.

13 January, 2017

Microsoft acquires Montreal-based Maluuba

On January 12 2017, Microsoft acquires Maluuba, one of the main startups for deep-learning in Montreal, starts their Microsoft Research Montreal Lab. Microsoft announces plans to expand the Maluuba Montreal office to have 80 engineers. A week after buying Maluuba, Québec’s premier Philippe Couillard, Québec’s Economic Minister Dominique Anglade, federal Industry Minister Navdeep Bains, and Microsoft president Brad Smith announce that Microsoft will double the size of its Montréal office within two years. The company also promises to give $6 million CAD to the Université de Montréal and $1 million CAD to McGill University. According to Premier Couillard “this commitment demonstrates that Quebec has not only been recognized for its hydroelectricity and its natural resources, but also for its skilled workforce.” The Microsoft announcement also confirmed that Yoshua Bengio will be advising Microsoft as well.

9 March, 2017

Google Cloud announces the opening of their first data centre in the greater Montreal area

The centre will go by the name "northamerica-northeast-1." The centre officially opens on February 1 2018.

22 March, 2017

The Canadian Liberal Government announces the $125 million Pan-Canadian AI strategy to be headed by CIFAR

Canada’s federal government announces it will provide $125 million CAD to launch the Pan-Canadian Artificial Intelligence Strategy to be delivered through CIFAR in order to “promote collaboration between Canada's main centres of expertise in Toronto–Waterloo, Montréal and Edmonton” and to “position Canada as a world-leading destination for companies seeking to invest in AI and innovation.” The federal government also announces the launch of Vector Institute, a non-profit research institute at University of Toronto, which “aims to put Canada at the centre of an emerging gold rush of innovation.”

22 March, 2017

The parliamentary budget announces the creation of the Superclusters Initiative

In order to respond to the problem that “too many Canadian companies believe that in order to grow, they must relocate outside of Canada, the parliamentary budget for 2017 announces the creation of the Superclusters Initiative and funding of $950 million CAD. The goal of the initiative is to "make it easier for innovators and potential customers to work closely together on research, development and demonstration activities that pursue major commercial opportunities, to boost productivity, and create jobs and drive economic growth" in Canada, with Montreal as one of the chosen locations.

20 April, 2017

IBM opens AI lab in Montreal

IBM opens an AI lab in Montreal to strengthen connections to MILA. Data science news reports state that IBM and Mila have been working together since at least July 2015.

15 May, 2017

Université de Montréal sets up the Artificial Intelligence (AI) Cluster Steering Committee at the request of the Quebec government

The mandate of the supercluster is to "make Quebec a leader in AI in research and innovation, business start-up and organizational AI adoption. The Université de Montréal provided the administrative services for the Steering Committee and supported its work overall. The 12-member Committee is co-chaired by Pierre Boivin, President and CEO of Claridge Inc., and Guy Breton, Rector of the Université de Montréal.” The research cluster receives $100 million over five years, with a goal to promote research and innovation, while developing related businesses

15 May, 2017

National Bank of Canada and IVADO form a partnership in Montreal

Through this agreement, the Bank and the Institute intend to "promote innovation in the financial services industry, support networking between universities and private companies in the field of artificial intelligence and encourage philanthropic analysis of data.”

18 May, 2017

HEC Launches the Creative Destructive Lab in Montreal

HEC Montréal partners with the Rotman School of Management at the University of Toronto to launch the Creative Destruction Lab, which is billed as "the world’s premier seed-stage program for massively scalable, science-based companies."

14 June, 2017

Element AI records $102 million in funding

US investors invest a record $102 million USD in Element AI.

14 June, 2017

eStruxture launches a new Canada-wide data center platform

This platform continues throughout the next years, beginning with the purchase of Netelligent Hosting Services and its data center operator (originally built in 2013) based in Montreal, which is renamed eStruxture MTL1. in December 2017, eStruxture acquires Montreal Gazette's former printing facility to develop another data center. In 2018, the company also acquires Kolotek from Gas Métro Plus. The South Shore data center is eStruxture's third site in their platform

17 August, 2017

Samsung starts an AI lab in Montreal

In the Samsung Advanced Institute of Technology, researchers "dispatched" from Korea will "work with local professors and students including Prof. Bengio to develop key algorithms and components for artificial intelligence such as voice/image recognition, translation, autonomous driving, and robots. It will also contribute to acquiring global talents and strengthening Samsung’s AI technology.” Ongoing collaboration between Bengio and Samsung started in 2014, but was physically solidified with the lab, which was set up in the J.-Armand Bombardier Incubator on the campus of Université de Montréal.

18 September, 2018

Microsoft relocates to Mile Ex

On September 18 2018, Microsoft announces to the world that they’re relocating to Mile Ex and “will be steps away from the new Montreal Institute for Learning Algorithms (MILA) and other AI research labs in the community.” Canada's Minister of Innovation, Science and Economic Development emphasize that “the Microsoft Research Montreal lab’s growth and new footprint will advance Montreal’s well-earned reputation as an AI hub, and will promote development and investment in the research community across Canada.”

15 September, 2017

Facebook opens an AI lab in Montreal, led by McGill professor Joelle Pineau

Facebook opens the FAIR AI lab, to be run by McGill professor Joelle Pineau. “The Montreal lab will house research scientists and engineers working on a wide range of ambitious AI research projects, but it will also have a special focus on reinforcement learning and dialog systems.” Alongside opening up an AI lab, they invested $7 million in the AI community at universities to nurture talent. Present at the Fair AI lab launch were Prime Minister Justin Trudeau, Mayor Denis Coderre and Quebec’s Innovation minister Dominique Anglade. Prior to this, Facebook launched its first Toronto office in Nov. 2009, in Vancouver in June 2013, and Montréal in July 2013 in order to “to better tap into business opportunities in Quebec” and “to serve businesses looking to expand their presence on the social network.”

27 September, 2017

Yoshua Bengio’s MILA is incorporated as a non-profit.

This incporation signals a shift from being a university-run centre to being its own private entity while still capitalizing on the advantages of being associated with a university.

6 October, 2017

DeepMind opens a lab in Montreal, led by Doina Precup

Doina Precup, Associate Professor in the School of Computer Science at McGill, Senior Fellow of the Canadian Institute for Advanced Research, and a member of MILA is selected to lead the team at DeepMind's Montreal lab. Doina’s expertise is in reinforcement learning. Precup planned to focus on fundamental research at McGill, MILA, and DeepMind. She will also maintain her role as Associate Professor at McGill. Deepmind also announces its intentions to “provide funding to support AI research, and also sponsorship of PhD students in order to support the Canadian AI ecosystem.

10 October, 2017

Thales creates an AI centre in Montreal

The AI lab will be called the Centre of Research and Technology in Artificial Intelligence eXpertise (cortAIx). Thales chose Montreal due to: “the wealth of scientists, PhD candidates and universities working in AI. Labor costs in the city are also about 30 per cent lower than in the U.S. and government subsidies makes the city an attractive investment, according to Keryer.” Thales planned to commit about $25 million to the centre over the following four to five years.

November, 2017

The Montreal Responsible AI Declaration is launched

The Montréal Responsible AI Declaration is launched as a project at the Forum on the Socially Responsible Development of Artificial Intelligence, organized by IVADO and Université de Montréal’s Centre de recherche en éthique, aiming to represent Montreal as a place where "ethical" and "responsible" AI happens, though nothing about the declaration is binding on Montreal AI companies. The event is organized by people such as Yoshua Bengio, David Decary-Hetu, and Doina Precup with funding from U de M, FRQSC, Palais de congres, CIFAR, Mila, IVADO, Google, and Montreal International. No social justice organizations or representatives of groups who have been harmed by the use of AI are involved.

21 November, 2017

RBC announces opening of the Borealis AI lab in Montreal

The announcement also states that McGill professor Jackie Cheung will be academic advisor to the lab. Borealis AI states it wants to collaborate with the Montreal Institute for Learning Algorithms. They also announce that RBC and the Bank of Montreal are injecting $4 million to the Creative Destruction Lab Montreal, which is a partnership between Université de Montréal’s HEC business school and U of T’s Rotman School of Management.

10 January, 2018

GI Partners acquires the Ericsson data centre

Prior to being acquired by GI Partners, the data centre was set to close when Ericsson announced a consolidation drive due to mounting losses. Ericsson reported its fourth consecutive quarter of losses, also announcing that sales were down six percent over the year before.

15 February, 2018

ISED announces that it is investing up to $950 million through the Innovation Superclusters Initiative

The $950 million investment announcement also confirms that each dollar is supposed to be “matched dollar for dollar by the private sector”, and is expected to “create more than 50,000 middle-class jobs and grow Canada’s economy by $50 billion over the next 10 years”. ISED also announces the 5 superclusters throughout the country, one of which is called the “SCALE.AI Supercluster” here in Quebec, seeking to “make Canada a world leading exporter by building intelligent supply chains through artificial intelligence and robotics”

27 March, 2018

Fonds de recherche du Québec, in partnership with Université de Montréal, held a workshop at Palais des congrès de Montréal on a project to create a world observatory on the social impacts of AI

The event featured 100 people from Quebec’s universities, representatives from government departments, civil society and the AI sector. It was help following the Forum on the Socially Responsible Development of Artificial Intelligence. It was announced that the observatory will recieve $5 million in funding to support its implementation. The FRQ also launched an application for proposals that included five institutions invovled in sector research and feature a "critical masss of researchers from teams, networks and groups that represent the FRQ's three sectors" with the sectors being Nature and Techology, Health, and Society and Culture. It is still unclear who applied and who was accepted into the observatory.

8 May, 2018

The Montreal AI Ethics Institute is launched

The Institute launches with a goal of researching the ethical, safe and inclusive development of AI.

May, 2018

Quebec’s Ministry of Economy, science and innovation launched a callout for requests to create an observatory on the social impacts of AI

The launch also specified that the program is to be funded through the province’s public academic research funding body “The initiative is funded by the Fonds de recherche du Québec (FRQ), in partnership with the ministère de l'Économie et de l'Innovation, in an amount of $1.5M per year over five years (total budget of $7.5M).”

May, 2018

The Quebec government announces the Strategy for the Development of Quebec’s Artificial Intelligence Ecosystem

MILA receives a $100 million investment over 5 years from the provincial government for the creation of an AI supercluster in Quebec. The strategy also details that in addition to the $100 million investment, the province will invest $102 million in AI projects: $60 million over 5 years to SCALE.AI for the implementation of the supercluster, $10 million over five years to launch the Creative Destruction Lab of Montreal, and Next AI. $5 million to help establish a new international AI organization in Montreal, $1 million so that applied mathematical science can be leveraged to support AI-related fields in Quebec, $12.5 to improve the Quebec computing infrastructure, $5 million for IVADO to carry out a Canada First research project on big-data, and $8.5 million to support collaborative AI research projects through prompt. The strategy also details investments in other industries that “could potentially have an impact on the development of the AI ecosystem,” such as tourism, manufacturing, business, and academia.

24 May, 2018

Havas announces AI innovation centre in Montreal

Paris-based communications firm Havas states that it plans to hire 60 employees by 2020. The centre is focussing on applied AI solutions to address client needs and challenges.

22 June, 2018

Microsoft moves members of FATE research group to Montreal

FATE is the Fairness, Accountability, Transparency, Ethics project run by Microsoft, that aims to develop computational techniques based on studies in sociology, history, science and technology.

28 July, 2018
